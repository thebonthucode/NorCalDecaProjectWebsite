Common Tax Mistakes to Avoid: A First-Timer's Guide

Date: March 17, 2025

Filing taxes for the first time can feel overwhelming, but avoiding a few common mistakes can make the process smoother and help you avoid costly errors. By being aware of these pitfalls and taking the right precautions, you can file your taxes with confidence and ensure everything goes as smoothly as possible. Here are some of the most common mistakes first-time filers make and how to avoid them.

One of the biggest mistakes you can make is filing your taxes late. Missing the tax deadline of April 15 can result in penalties and interest on any unpaid taxes. If you’re unable to file your return on time, you can request an extension, which gives you more time to complete your filing. However, it’s important to remember that an extension only gives you more time to file, not more time to pay. Your tax payments are still due by the original deadline, so be sure to pay any owed taxes on time to avoid additional penalties.

Another common mistake is ignoring available deductions and credits. Many first-time filers overlook valuable tax breaks, such as education credits, child care deductions, or even the standard deduction. These tax-saving opportunities can significantly reduce the amount of tax you owe. Be sure to research all the credits and deductions you qualify for, and don’t leave money on the table by failing to claim them. If you’re unsure about what you can claim, consider using tax software or consulting a professional to ensure you don’t miss anything important.

Incorrectly entering your personal information is another frequent mistake that can cause delays or issues with your return. Make sure your name, Social Security number, and bank account details are entered correctly. Even a small mistake, like a typo in your Social Security number or an incorrect account number for direct deposit, can result in delays in processing your return or receiving your refund. Double-check all personal information to ensure it’s accurate before submitting your tax return.

First-time filers also tend to forget to report all sources of income. Even if you earn a small amount from freelance work, side jobs, or gig economy work, you must report this income to the IRS. The IRS receives copies of 1099 forms, so if you leave any income off your return, it will likely be noticed. Reporting all income, no matter how small, helps you avoid penalties and ensures that your return is accurate and complete.

Finally, many people fail to keep proper records of their tax-related documents. It’s important to keep copies of your filed tax return and all supporting documents for at least three years, as the IRS may request them in the event of an audit. By staying organized and keeping accurate records, you can protect yourself and have the necessary documentation on hand if you need to refer to it later.

By avoiding these common mistakes, you can file your taxes with confidence and reduce the risk of delays, penalties, or audits. Paying attention to deadlines, claiming all available deductions and credits, ensuring accuracy with your personal information, reporting all income, and keeping proper records are essential steps for a successful tax filing experience. With the right preparation, you’ll be able to navigate tax season without unnecessary stress or confusion.
